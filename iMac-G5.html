The iMac G5

At the 2004 Apple Expo in Paris, the iMac G5 was announced. Since these personal computers had a heated output, the G5 used an advanced cooling system
controlled by the operating system. The low CPU loads silented the iMac. Apple prided itself for the "slimmest" desktop computer for sale at the time. The Mac came with a iSight webcam
mounted about the LCD display. This first Intel based Mac came in three models which varied between $1,299-$1,899 and would now be between $2,100-$3,000 today.
